Track Three

Men Reclaiming
Purpose

This may be the most important work I do. Purpose is not a luxury. It's the thing that determines what a man does the day he walks back into the world.

Who It's For

Men Who Haven't Had the Conversation That Changes Everything

Men inside correctional facilities often haven't had a single honest conversation in their lives about who they actually are, separate from what they've done.

Anthony is not there to pretend the past didn't happen. He's not there to motivate. He's there to have the conversations that almost no one will have:

  • What are you actually capable of?
  • What do you owe yourself?
  • Who do you want to be when you walk out?
  • What does it actually look like to live with purpose?

This work is for men who are incarcerated and ready to think seriously about who they are and who they're going to be. It's also for men in reentry, recently released, rebuilding, and in need of a real partner for that process.

You don't have to have it figured out. You just have to be willing to have the conversation.

reentry.jpg
The Programs

Three Phases. One Continuous Thread.

🔒

Inside the Walls

Facilitated group sessions delivered inside correctional facilities. These are real conversations about identity, purpose, and personal responsibility, not behavior modification programs. Anthony enters as a peer, not a counselor. He's earned the right to be direct.

  • Group cohorts of 8–15 men
  • Multi-session program format
  • Coordinated with facility admin
🌉

The Bridge Program

Designed for men in the 6–12 months before their release date. Focused on transition planning, not just logistics, but identity. Who is he walking out as? What's the plan? What support systems exist? Anthony builds this bridge before the gate opens.

  • Pre-release engagement (6–12 months out)
  • Identity and purpose work
  • Reentry planning and resource connection
🔑

Post-Release Coaching

One-on-one coaching for men who have been released and are rebuilding. The transition out is when most men are most vulnerable, and when consistent, honest coaching can make the most difference. Anthony is available for ongoing engagement post-release.

  • Regular 1:1 sessions
  • Accountability and follow-through
  • Available in-person (Florida)
Partnership & Access

How This Work Gets Into the Room

Access to correctional facilities requires partnership with facility administration and, often, with reentry organizations that already have established relationships inside. Anthony is actively building those partnerships in Florida.

If you work for or with a correctional facility, a reentry program, or a nonprofit serving formerly incarcerated men, Anthony wants to talk to you. The logistics are workable. The need is urgent.

"What are you capable of? What do you owe yourself? What happens when you get out? These are the conversations that determine what a man does with the rest of his life. I'm here for that conversation."

Funding Model

Grant-Eligible Programming

Track 3 programming is designed to be grant-eligible and can be structured to align with the funding requirements of foundations, government programs, and nonprofit sponsors focused on criminal justice reform and reentry support.

Funders looking to support evidence-based leadership and reentry programming in Florida are encouraged to reach out directly. Anthony is building the infrastructure to receive and deploy grant funding responsibly.

  • Eligible for criminal justice reform grants
  • Eligible for workforce development funding
  • Can be structured for corporate social responsibility partnerships
