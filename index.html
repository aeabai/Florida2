Who Is Anthony

The person who found his purpose by looking backward.

In his thirties, Anthony looked back at every job he'd ever held and found one thread running through all of it: developing people was what he loved most, and what he did best. He called it "Unleashing Genius." Everything since has been proof.

Over the next thirty years, he took that conviction into construction sites, boardrooms, youth programs, and project facilities on four continents — and he always left the room better than he found it.

In June 2026, he retired from Bechtel and moved to Florida to do the work he was always heading toward: three tracks, one philosophy, every person in the room mattering.

Anthony broke down barriers with a reluctant, newly-mobilized team in two days that would normally take months. His skill in this area is rare in the construction industry.

— John Andreatta, HLW Completions Manager · 40-Year Industry Veteran

How I Work
  • 01

    I give people what they need, not what they want.

    Growth lives on the other side of discomfort. I don't traffic in comfortable. I trade in honest — delivered with enough care that people can actually use it.

  • 02

    The work is personal, or it's nothing.

    I've sat in enough workshops where nobody changed. The difference is always the same: did the facilitator actually care about this person? I do. Every time.

  • 03

    Every person I work with has genius in them.

    Not potential. Not promise. Actual genius — specific to who they are. My job is to help them find it, trust it, and use it. That's it.

  • 04

    I'm present or I'm not there.

    Full presence is the most underrated tool in coaching. When someone knows you're actually in the room with them — not just performing — everything changes.
