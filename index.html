Who Is Anthony

I found my purpose by looking inward and backward

In my thirties, I looked back at every job I had ever held and found one thread running through all of it: developing people was what I loved most and what I did best. The real shift came when I stopped treating that ability as something accidental and became intentional about it. That realization led me to start my first coaching firm in Houston, Texas, where I worked with small business owners and young entrepreneurs. I called the work "Unleashing Genius" and everything since has been proof.

Over the next thirty years, I carried that belief into construction sites, boardrooms, youth programs, and project facilities on five continents. No matter the setting, the goal stayed the same: leave people better than I found them.

In July 2026, I retired from Bechtel and moved to Florida to reconnect with my youngest brother and focus fully on the work I was always moving toward: three tracks, one philosophy, every person mattering.

The foundation of everything I believe about growth and development is this: to become a better leader, coach, mentor, parent, partner, friend, or neighbor, you must first become a better human being. Because who you are shows up in everything you do. You will never become a more impactful anything if you are not authentically that thing at your core.

How I Work
  • 01

    I give people what they need, not what they want.

    Growth lives on the other side of discomfort. I don't traffic in comfortable. I trade in honest, delivered with enough care that people can actually use it.

  • 02

    The work is personal, or it's nothing.

    I've sat in enough workshops where nobody changed. The difference is always the same: did the facilitator actually care about this person? I do. Every time.

  • 03

    Every person I work with has genius in them.

    Not potential. Not promise. Actual genius, specific to who they are. My job is to help them find it, trust it, and use it. That's it.

  • 04

    I'm present or I'm not there.

    Full presence is the most underrated tool in coaching. When someone knows you're actually in the room with them, not just performing, everything changes.
