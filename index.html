The Story Behind the Work

I wasn't always the guy who helped people grow.

Thirty years ago I was good at my job and terrible with people. I knew how to get things done. I didn't always know — or care — who I ran over doing it. Then a man named David Foster sat me down and told me I was the "go-to last guy." Effective. Dependable. But leaving a trail of damaged relationships behind me.

"You're the guy we call last — because by then, the damage is already done."

That conversation changed my life. Not because it was gentle — it wasn't. Because it was true.

I spent the next thirty years figuring out how to be the kind of leader who gets results AND leaves people better than I found them. I delivered over a thousand presentations across five continents. I coached executives who couldn't talk to their own teams. I sat with kids at Covenant House who'd been thrown away by the world. I learned something that I now know to be true without exception:

Genius isn't rare. It's buried. By circumstance. By fear. By the story someone told them about who they were.

My job — my purpose — is to help people unbury it. I've been doing it my whole career. Now I'm doing it full-time, for anyone who's ready. No matter where they're starting from.

How I Work
  • 01

    I give people what they need, not what they want.

    Growth lives on the other side of discomfort. I don't traffic in comfortable. I trade in honest — delivered with enough care that people can actually use it.

  • 02

    The work is personal, or it's nothing.

    I've sat in enough workshops where nobody changed. The difference is always the same: did the facilitator actually care about this person? I do. Every time.

  • 03

    Every person I work with has genius in them.

    Not potential. Not promise. Actual genius — specific to who they are. My job is to help them find it, trust it, and use it. That's it.

  • 04

    I'm present or I'm not there.

    Full presence is the most underrated tool in coaching. When someone knows you're actually in the room with them — not just performing — everything changes.
